Health Tips & Guidance

Practical Ayurvedic advice for common health concerns based on your unique constitution.

Managing Diabetes Naturally

Blood Sugar Balance

Key Recommendations

  • Bitter is better: Include bitter gourd, fenugreek, neem, and turmeric in your diet
  • Timing matters: Eat your largest meal at lunch when digestive fire is strongest
  • Movement: Walk for 30 minutes after meals to improve glucose metabolism
  • Herbal support: Try our Madhuhara tea blend with jamun, gurmar, and daruharidra
Dietary Guidelines
  • Favor bitter, astringent, and pungent tastes
  • Reduce sweet, sour, and salty foods
  • Use ghee moderately for internal lubrication
  • Soak 1 tsp fenugreek seeds overnight, drink water in morning
Lifestyle Practices
  • Practice Kapalbhati pranayama daily
  • Self-massage with sesame oil weekly
  • Maintain regular sleep schedule
  • Manage stress through meditation

Ayurvedic Lifestyle

Daily Routines (Dinacharya)

Align your daily activities with nature's rhythms for optimal health and vitality.

Daily Routine for Children

Establishing healthy habits early sets the foundation for lifelong wellbeing.

6-8 AM

Morning Rituals

  • Wake up naturally with the sun
  • Drink warm water with honey (1+ year)
  • Gentle stretching or yoga poses
  • Oil massage for infants and toddlers
  • Bath with calming herbs like chamomile
8-9 AM

Breakfast

  • Fresh seasonal fruits
  • Warm milk with turmeric or almonds
  • Whole grain porridge with ghee
  • Nuts and seeds (soaked for better digestion)
12-2 PM

Midday

  • Main meal with all six tastes
  • Include rice, dal, vegetables, and ghee
  • Encourage mindful eating without distractions
  • Short rest after lunch if possible
4-6 PM

Evening Activities

  • Outdoor play in nature
  • Creative activities like drawing or music
  • Light snack like fruits or nuts
  • Family time and storytelling
7-9 PM

Night Routine

  • Light dinner by 7 PM
  • Warm milk with nutmeg for better sleep
  • Calming activities like reading
  • Bedtime by 9 PM for proper growth
